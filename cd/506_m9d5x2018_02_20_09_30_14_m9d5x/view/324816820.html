Summary
Kenya and Tanzania have underscored the need to eliminate non-tariff barriers and ease market access for each other's products and services as a means enhancing bilateral trade. At a meeting held in Mombasa, the two partner states deliberated on trade related concerns ranging from multiple charges on levies, lack of preferential treatment, delays, inspection fees, non-payment of suppliers by Uchumi and Nakumatt supermarkets, slow customs procedures and slow implementation of the relevant East African Community directives among others. The Republic of Kenya and United Republic of Tanzania agreed to continuously engage in regard to the implementing the Single Customs Territory system (KRA and TRA). KEBS and TFDA will cooperate on harmonisation of SPS measures.
