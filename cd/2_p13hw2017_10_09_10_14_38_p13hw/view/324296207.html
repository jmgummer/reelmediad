Agency banking spells doom for tellers as lenders cut 2,517 jobs


Sunday Standard

27-Aug-2017

Type : Weekend Newspaper

Journalist : Patrick Alushula

Page : 35

AVE : 489237

Summary
Alternative service channels elbowed out a record 2,517 workers from the banking halls in 2016 as management moved to break the expensive brick and mortar model. While banks added 17 staff to the management level, 628 supervisory jobs were lost while clerical and secretarial level lost 1,988 jobs. However, 82 support staff jobs were created, according to the Bank Supervision Annual Report 2016 by the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK). Job cuts have been announced at KCB, Standard Chartered, Barclays, Family Bank, National Bank of Kenya, NIC Bank, Ecobank, Bank of Africa, First Community Bank and Sidian Bank. Equity Bank CEO James Mwangi who cut 200 jobs in the South Sudan subsidiary alone has already stressed that he will only retain branches that can serve high value transactions and busi- ness advisory roles.
